    • Youth Ministry

    The Youth Ministry in the Kingstown/ Chateaubelair circuit is dedicated to enabling and encouraging youths to know God at an early age and to be steadfast in their walk with God. We strongly believe youth is the best age to be rooted in Christ, and so we aim to provide them with best leaders who can advise and encourage them in their first steps.

    The Youth Ministry targets all youths through the following groups:

    Youth Fellowship – Members of the Youth Fellowship can engage in weekly devotion and bible study, and visitation. In each church, every third(3rd) Sunday is Youth Sunday, where the members of the Youth Group are encouraged to conduct the service, read the scripture lessons, sing in the Youth Choir, and express their talents such as in song, dance or playing instruments. Groups are also encouraged to do fun activities throughout the year such as participating in Youth Socials, Fun Walks, spiritual debates and much more.

    The Youth Commission is the main organisation in the circuit responsible for planning and maintaining youth activities throughout the year. It is coordinator of the annual Youth Month in the circuit. Youth Month is celebrated in the second month of the year, February. Youth Month allows all youths in the circuit to come together to showcase their talents in a wide range of friendly competitions including Dance, Choir, Duet, Drama, Public Speaking, and Quiz. Youth Socials are also organized by the Commission to allow the youths from across to circuit to meet, mingle, and form friendships with other Christian youths.

    The Youth Commission also hosts the Circuit Youth Camp every year. For more information, see our page dedicated to Youths.

    Other groups in the church which seek to help youths are:
    1. The Youth Choir
    2. The Young Adults Group
    3. The Children's Ministry

    Women's League

    The Women's League seeks to bring women in each congregation together to worship God by singing songs of praises, sharing the Scripture, and congregating for steadfast prayer. One of the strongest roles of the league is to visit the sick/shut-in and sing hymns of upliftment to them. Overall, the purpose of the Women's League is to provide weekly spiritual inspiration for women.

    The Women's League is created under the umbrella organization of MCCA Women. According to our connexion's website, "all female members of the MCCA are members of MCCA Women. MCCA Women is a constituent part of the MCCA and aims to enable women of the MCCA to deepen their personal commitment to Jesus and to foster fellowship among the women of the Conference Area.

    "An assembly of all MCCA women is held very five years. The first assembly was held in 1980 at Codrington College in Barbados. The fourth Lord’s Day (Sunday) in June every year is celebrated as MCCA Women’s Lord’s Day, usually under the theme, "BAMBELELA! Never Give Up, Christ is Our Hope!"

    Men's Fellowship

    Similar to the Women's League, through the Men's Fellowship, men gather together to fellowship during the week, visit the sick, and share their testimonies. It falls under the MCCA umbrella organisation, MCCA Men. "The Connexional Council at its November 2004 meeting in Belize City, Belize, confirmed the agreement that efforts would be put in place to have an established connexional umbrella organization for men, similar and parallel to the MCCA Women. This effort formed part of the Connexional Conference's vision for there to be three (3) Connexional umbrella organizations - one each for Men, Women and Youth.

    "The existence of an MCCA Men's organization will therefore provide to recognized entities, internal and external to the church, reliable opportunities for linkages through which appropriate resources may be accessed for training and development of the male members of the MCCA," states the MCCA website.

    Bible Study

    “But he answered and said, It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God (Matthew 4:4)." In every Christian’s life, reading the Bible should be of high priority. God’s Word transforms and equip us with the knowledge and discipline to overcome the struggles that we face daily. This is why we encourage persons to attend weekly Bible Study where members or visitors can come together in fellowship, and can analyse chosen passages, learn more about more about the author and history behind the text, the meaning of the passage, and understand how its teachings are applicable in their daily life. Members and visitors are also free to ask their burning questions here. Bible Study is available at every church. For more information on times, click here.

    Classes

    Classes in the Kingstown/Chateaubelair Circuit are small fellowship groups where members can meet, read the Word together, visit the Sick/Shut-in Members, give to the community, raise funds for the furtherance of God's Kingdom, and much more. Each church has a maximum of 12 classes. Class members are sometimes arranged by age and address. Contact a Congregational Steward of your church and ask to join a class today! Find out more about our Class System by contacting the Church Office at 457-1989 or kingchat@vincysurf.com.
